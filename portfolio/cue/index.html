Real-vehicle experiment: Interaction Cue System in Highly Automated Vehicle

This ongoing project aims at exploring the general driving experience in a highly automated vehicle.

   

Research Goal

We would like to explore the driver’s experience in a level 3 automated driving vehicle while he or she is conducting the non-driving related tasks

  • We define the automation surprise in the automotive field. We are wondering whether the driver would encounter automation surprise when doing non-driving related tasks in an on-road highly automated vehicle. A prototype would be designed to test if an implicit cue system could help reduce automation surprise without disturbing the driver.

   

Experiment design

  • The experiment would be conducted with On-road real vehicle.
  • Wizard of Oz method is applied to the experiment. The interior experiment car was modified for the experiment to create the realism of level 3 automated driving.
  • A within-subjects experiment would be conducted, that is, half of the participants would have the controlled group without the prototype and half of the participants would experience the scenario with the prototype.
  • At least 20 participants would engage in this experiment.
