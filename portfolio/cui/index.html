Conversitional User Interface of Automated Vehicles

This project explored on the Coversitional User Interface(CUI) and its intonation influence on trust level for Automated Vehicles(AVs). I worked on this project independently at TU/e supervised by dr. P.A.M. Ruijten-Dodoiu.

   

Research Question

“Does the intonation of a Conersitional User Interface influence the trust from users for the autonomous vehicle?”

  • We assume the speech with rising intonation would be perceived as a question and less trustworthy.

   

Conversitonal User Interface

The speech of CUI was directly generated by Adobe Audition(Adobe Inc., San Jose, California). There are 6 speech sentences, which are in male voice and female voice with falling and rising intonation, that is, a total of 24 speech files were generated. All the speeches were designed based on the tasks that may happen in the scenario.

   

Experiment design

  • The experiment was conducted with Driving Simulator.
  • Unity was used to simulate the autonomous driving scenario instead of playing a video of the highway driving scene. The unity could simulate the real situation of driving, where the events will not stay the same in different rounds of user experiencing the scenario. It helped the participants generate the feeling of realistic autonomous driving.
  • Wizard of Oz method is applied to CUI.
  • A 2 (intonation: raising vs falling) x 2 (gender of voice: male vs female) x2(focusing:system vs CUI) mixed design will be used to study the research question. Intonation is manipulated within-subjects, with all the participants being tested with both the rising and falling intonation. Gender of voice and the aspect where the participants focusing is manipulated between-subjects.
  • 12 participants engaged in this experiment.

   

Result

  • Quantitative Data Cronbach alpha was calculated for each instrument. Quatitative data was collected from the Likert-scale questionniares filled by the participants after experiencing each scenario.

  • Qualitative Data was collected from the semi-structured interview. The result from interview really surprised me and gave me an insight for furture design. Participants gave me answers against my assumption but make it own sense. For example, most participants did notice the rising intonation but did not perceive it as a interrogative tone. They trusted the AVs with rising intonation CUI because it was perceived more friendly and natural. On the contrary, the other parts of participants felt the AVs with falling intonation CUI more trustworthy because the robotic voice gave them the sense of the computer was functioning. They trusted less on the perceived naturally interface as they believed the computer and machine was more dependable than human according to the interview.
